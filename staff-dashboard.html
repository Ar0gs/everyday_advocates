Service Boundaries — Internal Reference

⏳
Checking your acknowledgement status…

Critical principle: When in doubt about whether something falls within our scope, the answer is always to stop, check, and ask — never to improvise. If a situation arises that this document does not cover, escalate to the founder immediately.

What Everyday Advocates is

Everyday Advocates is a non-regulated social support service. We are not a care agency, a healthcare provider, a legal firm, or a financial services company. We provide social connection and companionship, practical help with life admin, technology support, and (as service lines expand) transition support and coaching. We are not regulated by the CQC, but we are bound by UK GDPR, safeguarding duties, insurance obligations, and our own standards.

Companionship & Wellbeing Visits

We do: friendly regular visits, meaningful conversation, walks and outings, light emotional support, wellbeing check-ins, and prompt reporting of concerns.

We do not: provide counselling, therapy, or mental health treatment; act as a substitute for professional mental health care; make promises about contact frequency outside agreed visits; develop personal relationships with clients outside work; accept gifts of significant value; or share personal contact details.

Emotional support boundary

Advocates may listen and offer reassurance. They must not attempt to counsel, diagnose, or treat emotional or mental health difficulties. If a client discloses significant distress, self-harm, or suicidal thoughts — follow the Safeguarding Protocol immediately. Do not attempt to manage this alone.

Life Admin & Support

We do: help reading official letters and documents, support completing forms (benefits, NHS, housing, council, pension), book and chase appointments, organise paperwork, and liaise with organisations with written consent.

We do not: provide legal or financial advice, handle client cash/cards/bank transfers, access bank accounts even with permission, sign documents on a client's behalf, or make decisions for the client.

Financial boundary — absolute

No advocate may ever handle client money, access bank accounts, or conduct financial transactions on behalf of a client under any circumstances. If a client requests this, explain it falls outside our service and offer to help them contact their bank, family member, or a regulated financial adviser. Any attempt to pressure an advocate into handling client finances must be reported to the founder immediately.

Tech Support for Seniors

We do: set up smartphones/tablets already owned by the client, teach WhatsApp/FaceTime/video calling, help navigate the NHS App and online services, teach safe internet use and scam awareness, help with device settings and accessibility, and provide follow-up support in subsequent sessions.

We do not: purchase devices or accounts on a client's behalf, access client email/social media/accounts without them present, set up accounts using the advocate's own details, provide IT support beyond consumer devices, diagnose or repair hardware, or provide ongoing remote support outside booked sessions.

Scam awareness

If an advocate suspects a client has been targeted by a scam — or is about to be — they must raise this with the founder immediately. Do not contact the scammer, intervene in financial transactions, or advise specific financial action. Signpost to Action Fraud (0300 123 2040) and notify the family with client consent.

Wellbeing Check-Ins

We do: note and record changes in mood/behaviour/presentation, ask open and gentle questions, record observations in the Session Summary Form after every visit, escalate concerns promptly, and signpost to appropriate support.

We do not: conduct formal mental health assessments, diagnose any physical or mental health condition, administer or remind clients to take medication, provide clinical health advice, or contact a client's GP without founder approval.

Everyday Transitions & Everyday Futures — coming soon

These service lines are in development. Do not offer or describe these services to clients until formally launched. Hospital to Home Transition will never involve personal care, medication administration, or clinical decision-making — the moment a request crosses into clinical territory, escalate immediately. Retirement Transition Coaching is forward-looking and growth-focused, not therapy — if a client presents with significant mental health difficulties during coaching, pause and refer them to their GP or a mental health professional.

Universal boundaries — all service lines

Safeguarding: All advocates have a duty to report any safeguarding concern — this overrides client confidentiality. If there is immediate risk to life, call 999 first, then notify the founder. Suspected abuse, neglect, or exploitation must be reported to the founder the same day. Coventry Safeguarding Adults Board: 024 7683 3003.

Lone worker safety: Always inform the founder of visit address, time, and expected duration before a visit. Check in after every visit — if a check-in is missed, the founder must attempt contact within 30 minutes. If you feel unsafe at any point, leave and contact the founder immediately — no explanation required.

Confidentiality: All client information is strictly confidential. Client information must not be stored on personal devices. Photography of clients or their homes is prohibited without explicit written consent. Social media posts mentioning clients — even without names — are prohibited.

Professional conduct: Advocates must not accept cash payments, accept loans or gifts over nominal value, be named in a client's will, form personal relationships with clients or their families, or share personal social media details. If a client attempts contact outside the service, notify the founder immediately.

Physical boundaries: Advocates must not perform personal care tasks (washing, dressing, toileting, wound care) or lift/physically assist a client with mobility — this is a regulated care task. If a client falls and is injured, call 999 — do not attempt to lift them. Advocates must not drive clients in personal vehicles without specific insurance and founder approval.

Escalation protocol

When a boundary situation arises: Stop — do not proceed; Stabilise — ensure the client is safe; Contact the founder — same day, without exception; Document — record in the Incident Log or Lessons Learned Log; Do not discuss with family or third parties until advised by the founder.

When to call 999 first

Medical emergency (stroke, heart attack, collapse, serious injury), immediate risk of harm to anyone, or fire/gas leak/other immediate physical danger. In all these cases: 999 first, founder second.
